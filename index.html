Why Water & Sanitation Matter

Access to clean water and proper sanitation is fundamental for good health. Lack of these essentials contributes to the spread of diseases such as cholera, diarrhea, and typhoid. Sustainable solutions can save millions of lives annually.

Clean Water

Ensures hydration, supports agriculture, and prevents waterborne illnesses.

Sanitation

Proper waste disposal reduces environmental contamination and disease spread.

Hygiene

Handwashing and good hygiene practices are the simplest and most effective health interventions.

WASH Factors & Health Risks

Factor Impact Related Health Risks
Unsafe Drinking Water Exposure to pathogens Cholera, Diarrhea, Typhoid
Poor Sanitation Contaminated environment Parasitic Infections, Dysentery
Lack of Hygiene Hand-to-mouth transmission Respiratory Illnesses, Gastroenteritis
Open Defecation Spread of fecal matter Diarrheal Disease, Soil-Transmitted Helminths
